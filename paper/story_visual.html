Two prices for the same question

A rigorous storyboard in the spirit of patient math videos—open this file in a browser and step through the scenes.

Scene 0 — Hook
Polymarket quotes a Yes price for “Bitcoin above K at date T.” Binance lists a European call with the same strike and expiry. Under standard no-arbitrage pricing, that call implies a model fair value for the binary payoff. The paper asks whether those two numbers match on average when you line up clocks and strikes.
Scene 1 — The random payoff
Let ST be Bitcoin at resolution. The Polymarket contract pays 1 if ST > K, else 0. Under an equivalent martingale measure Q (used for pricing derivatives), the time‑t value of that payoff, discounted at the riskless rate r, is

e−rτ · Q(ST > K | ℱt).

This is not claiming Q equals real-world frequencies; it is the pricing measure encoded in traded options.
Scene 2 — Black–Scholes links call to binary
Assume under Q, log ST is normal with mean and variance determined by (St, r, σ, τ). Then Q(ST > K) = Φ(d₂) for the usual d₂. The European call formula C = SΦ(d₁) − K e−rτ Φ(d₂) contains the same Φ(d₂) term. Hence the discounted binary value is Pfair(σ) = e−rτ Φ(d₂(σ)). The paper’s Pfair,t is this quantity evaluated at the volatility that makes the model call match the observed Binance call each hour.
Scene 3 — The estimand and the gap
At each aligned hour t, Ppoly,t is the traded Polymarket Yes probability. Define Dt = Ppoly,t − Pfair,t. The primary estimand is E[Dt] (under the sampling scheme induced by the panel). Interpretation: a systematically positive average means Polymarket’s Yes is usually richer than the option-implied benchmark, after alignment—not necessarily “wrong,” but not equal in expectation.

Scene 4 — Inference (what “significant” means here)
Classical t-test: tests mean Dt = 0 assuming i.i.d. Gaussian noise (idealized). HAC (Newey–West) interval: same mean, but standard errors allow Dt to be correlated across hours—important at hourly frequency. Block bootstrap: resamples contiguous blocks to preserve serial dependence when approximating the sampling distribution of the sample mean. The paper reports that all three point away from zero for the flagship and pooled samples: the positive average gap is not an artifact of picking one variance formula.
Scene 5 — Numbers you should carry away
Flagship September 2023 contract: n = 214 hours, mean gap ≈ 0.0558 (about 5.6 percentage points on the probability scale). Pooled three contracts: n = 287, mean gap ≈ 0.0633. Caveat (always): Pfair is model-built (Black–Scholes, one σ per hour, archive data). Disagreement measures distance to that benchmark, not “objective truth.”
